Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist. She was born in Warsaw, Poland in 1867. Curie moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne University. She conducted research on radioactivity and discovered the elements polonium and radium.

Marie Curie married Pierre Curie, a French physicist. Together they won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903. She later won a second Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911. Their daughter Irene Joliot-Curie also became a famous scientist.

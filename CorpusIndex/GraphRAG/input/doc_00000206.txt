"Jonathan Larson"
Jonathan Larson Jonathan David Larson (February 4, 1960 – January 25, 1996) was an American composer and playwright noted for exploring the social issues of multiculturalism, addiction, and homophobia in his work. Typical examples of his use of these themes are found in his works ""Rent"" and ""tick, tick... BOOM!"" He received three posthumous Tony Awards and a posthumous Pulitzer Prize for Drama for the rock musical ""Rent"".
